Pilestredet 35

Ellen Gleditsch's building

General info

Situated in a central part of Oslo, Pilestredet 35 is OsloMet's largest building. It is the main building of several courses, including Information Technology, Computerengineering and Applied Computer Technology.

At 8 stories plus a basement, the building offers a good amount of classrooms, auditoriums, study spaces, and labs for students. The building also houses a large canteen provided by SiO, as well as a library and the Makerspace where students can use tools provided by the university to create. Additionally, the building contains a gym in the basement, as well as a rock climbing wall in building's main hall.

Ellen Gleditsch

Pilestredet 35 is named after the chemist Ellen Gleditsch (1879-1968) who worked as Marie Curie's assistant and was a professor at the University of Oslo. Gleditsch was also a prominent voice for women's rights.

Transportation

The building is easily accessible by several modes of transport. Several tram lines stop just outside the building, and there's only a short walk to either bus or metro. The building's central location also means that it is easily reached by foot from most of Oslo city centre.
